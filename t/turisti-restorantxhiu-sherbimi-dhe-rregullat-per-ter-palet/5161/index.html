Te gjithe u njohem kete dite me nje ngjarje te pazakonshme per turizmin shqiptar. Te themi te drejten, te gjithe ne e denuam pronarin apo restorantxhiun qe na kujtoi filmat horror hollivudian. Mirpo keto dite, pas denimit te restorantxhiut, na erdhen vetvetiu disa pyetje.

  • Pse duhej te shkonte puna deri ne gjykate ?
    A nuk kishte (ka) ne Shqiperi, organe lokale qe nderhyjne per te zgjidhur konflikte ndermjet klientit dhe restorantit.
  • Si u gjend ai hipur ne makine jashte ?
    Kjo pike zbardh edhe dyshimet e njerzeve dhe hedh drite mbi te verteten, se tani me mbrapa po ndigjojme edhe te tjere qe thone se restorantxhiu nuk kishte faj. E ne rast se ai nuk paska faj, cila ishte zanafilla e konfliktit.
    Mendja ma thote se shkaku mund te kete qene fatura, ku per fat te keq, ne mbi 50% e restoranteve, kur porosit shume artikuj/produkte, restorantet ta fusin ne çmim dhe 1-2 produkte me shume. Kjo me ka ndodhur mua dhe shume te tjerve.
    Konflikti ka degjeneruar ne sharje, dhe si perfundim kemi vidion makabre, ku kam pershtypjen se, edhe vete restoranxhiu po ta shohi tani, do habitet me skizofrenine e tij te atyre momonteve.
    Mirpo lind nje pyetje tjeter;
  • Valle ne kete konflikt, kemi vetem kete vidion skandaloze, qe na ul ne syte e te huajve, dhe kush ishte i interesuar per nje skandal te tille, per nje pamje makabre.
    Te thuash Spanja, jo nuk e besoj, se ne nuk hyjme fare ne refene. Spanja e ka konkurrencen me Francen, dhe jane dy vendet kampione te turizmit. Te thuash ndonje vend rrotull nesh, mundet, nuk perjashtohet si opsion, pas rritjes se turizmit ne vendin tone, mirpo lind pyetja;
  • Cili ishte roli i dy shqiptarve ne momentin e restorantit dhe roli i spanjollve, kush e agravoi me shume situaten.
    A u hulumtua nga organet e ndryshme shqiptare kjo gje, apo hajde te marrim kopanet dhe te veme gjoba stratosferike, gjoba qe fatkeqesisht jane shume me te larta se ketu ne perendim dhe qe e stresojne shoqerine shqiptare.
    Dhe se fundi, si ne gjithe boten, a i kane restorantet shqiptare te afishuara çmimet dhe rregullat, se shihet qe shume restorante nuk i kane te afishuara ne mur rregullat.
    Mendoj se kjo ngjarje, nxorri ne drite shume te meta te turizmit tone, si dhe punen qe na pret ne te ardhmen per ta permisuar kete sektor, por jo per ta ndeshkuar makabrisht siç veproi restoranxhiu budalla.

“There is terror in numbers,” writes Darrell Huff in How to Lie with Statistics . And nowhere does this terror translate to blind acceptance of authority more than in the slippery world of averages, correlations, graphs, and trends. Huff sought to break through “the daze that follows the collision of statistics with the human mind” with this slim volume, first published in 1954. The book remains relevant as a wake-up call for people unaccustomed to examining the endless flow of numbers pouring from Wall Street, Madison Avenue, and everywhere else someone has an axe to grind, a point to prove, or a product to sell. “The secret language of statistics, so appealing in a fact-minded culture, is employed to sensationalize, inflate, confuse, and oversimplify,” warns Huff.

Although many of the examples used in the book are charmingly dated, the cautions are timeless. Statistics are rife with opportunities for misuse, from “gee-whiz graphs” that add nonexistent drama to trends, to “results” detached from their method and meaning, to statistics’ ultimate bugaboo–faulty cause-and-effect reasoning. Huff’s tone is tolerant and amused, but no-nonsense. Like a lecturing father, he expects you to learn something useful from the book, and start applying it every day. Never be a sucker again, he cries!

Even if you can’t find a source of demonstrable bias, allow yourself some degree of skepticism about the results as long as there is a possibility of bias somewhere. There always is.

Read How to Lie with Statistics . Whether you encounter statistics at work, at school, or in advertising, you’ll remember its simple lessons. Don’t be terrorized by numbers, Huff implores. “The fact is that, despite its mathematical base, statistics is as much an art as it is a science.” –Therese Littleton
